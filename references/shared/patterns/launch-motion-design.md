# Pattern: Launch Motion Design

## Applicable Situation

Use when planning a product, feature, campaign, or market-entry launch from a GTM perspective.

## Inputs

- Target audience
- Offer or product surface
- Positioning
- Channel options
- Proof assets
- Follow-up motion

## Default Workflow

1. Define the launch objective: awareness, pipeline, activation, conversion, expansion, or category education.
2. Choose the audience and buyer champion.
3. Match the message to the channel and buyer stage.
4. Select proof assets: customer story, demo, benchmark, comparison, template, or founder POV.
5. Design the follow-up motion before launch: outbound, sales assist, retargeting, community, partner, or content loop.
6. Define success metrics and learning questions.

## Branch Rules

- If channels are noisy, prioritize trust signals and defensible loops.
- If the launch is for an AI-native product, explain why the product is useful now and what changed in the market.
- If Product work is not ready, define GTM prerequisites and stop short of writing a PRD.

## Anti-Patterns

- Launching with a generic announcement and no follow-up motion.
- Treating distribution as an afterthought after product work is complete.
- Measuring only impressions or traffic when the goal is pipeline or conversion.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The launch has a named audience, message, channel, proof, and follow-up.
- Metrics match the launch objective.
- The plan distinguishes GTM readiness from Product readiness.

## Conflict Notes

Launch advice often mixes product, brand, and growth. Keep this pattern focused on GTM motion; Product readiness is a prerequisite, not the deliverable.
